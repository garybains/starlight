What is sleep training?

  • Home
  • Blog Post 1

When I first started telling people that I was training to be a sleep consultant, I got a lot of raised eyebrows, but also a lot of people saying, “I could’ve used your help ‘X’ years ago.” As for many things in life, there is more than one school of thought. It seems in this case, that either people know what you do and wish you had been there to help them in the past or they THINK they know what you do and think you just tell people to let babies cry to sleep.

Sleep training, consulting: In MY belief, it is a look at the bigger picture. I take a look at your parenting style, your family situation and support system, baby’s temperament, nutrition, sleep environment, wind-down routine, what you have tried already, what you are willing to try, where the hiccups lie in your little one’s current sleep situation, and how we can catch our breath together! I have been trained in many different sleep strategies and know each baby and family has a strategy or two that will fit them.

I create a custom plan based on the above and explain the reasons behind what we are doing. Most people are reluctant to just jump in unless they understand why we are doing what we are doing. I teach families, why certain things are the way they are. I work with a family, but also need the family to work with me because it’s more than reading a document, its setting the wheels in motion. By taking the time to work at getting baby to sleep, you start to see improvements. It’s not always smooth sailing, there are colds, time changes, teething and siblings that make things interesting, but there is always improved sleep for not only baby but the sleep deprived family as well.
